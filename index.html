01
UI/UX Design · Figma

Nike Landing Page — Dual Concept Study

Exploring contrasting design languages for the world's most iconic sportswear brand

+
Client / Context
Self-initiated study — Nike (practice project)
Tools Used
Figma, Google Fonts
Role
Solo UI Designer
OBJECTIVE

To explore how the same brand (Nike) can be expressed through two completely different visual systems — one minimal and premium (Air Jordan), one bold and athletic (Air Max) — and demonstrate adaptability to client briefs.

PROCESS

Researched Nike's brand guidelines and competitor landing pages. For Air Jordan: chose a warm neutral background (#c4b9b0 tone), oversized display typography, and a reflective product placement to create a luxury sneaker feel. For Air Max: inverted to dark mode, italic heavy type, geometric circle backgrounds, and electric accents to communicate gym performance energy. Each layout prioritized a single CTA ("Add to Cart") above the fold.

KEY DECISIONS

Typography was the primary differentiator. Air Jordan used thin uppercase serif-adjacent lettering to feel high-end. Air Max used bold italic grotesque to feel powerful. Product image placement was intentional — Jordan floating compositionally, Air Max at an aggressive angle.

WHAT I'D IMPROVE NOW

Add user flow documentation, mobile responsive frames, and hover/interaction states. The Air Max body text is too small at mobile scale. The Air Jordan product reflection shadow could be more refined.

PORTFOLIO DESCRIPTION

"A dual-concept Figma study exploring how visual language shapes brand perception — demonstrating that design isn't one-size-fits-all, even within the same brand identity."

02
Graphic Design · Institutional Client

P.C. Jabin Science College — Event Poster Series

Official communications for AI, 3D Printing, and Science events

+
Client
KLE Society's P.C. Jabin Science College, Hubballi
Tools Used
Canva (poster design + branding)
Role
Graphic Designer
OBJECTIVE

Design a series of event promotion posters for official college events — AI & ML course launch, 3D Printing workshop, and Sustainable Energy seminar. Posters needed to carry institutional branding (KLE crest, college name, address) while being visually engaging for a student audience.

PROCESS

Each poster adapted to its event's tone: the AI poster used a vibrant purple-to-blue gradient with a friendly robot illustration to make technology approachable. The 3D Printing poster used a space/galaxy aesthetic with bold mixed-type hierarchy. The Science event used a green-themed nature aesthetic to reinforce the sustainability message. All followed the college's brand identity constraints.

CHALLENGES

Working within institutional constraints (mandatory crest, full address, specific resource person names) while keeping designs visually dynamic. Balancing information density with visual appeal for a student audience that scrolls quickly.

WHAT I'D IMPROVE NOW

The 3D Printing poster has a critical typo ("WROKSHOP") — in a professional context this would be a major quality-control failure. Always have a second pair of eyes proofread before final export. I'd also create more visual consistency across the series by establishing a shared design system (consistent crest placement, font pairing, footer treatment).

PORTFOLIO DESCRIPTION

"Institutional graphic design for a real client with real constraints — balancing brand compliance, information hierarchy, and audience engagement across a multi-event poster series."

03
Graphic Design · Music Industry

DEADEND — Single Release Artwork for Saana

Editorial music cover art for an independent artist's Spotify release

+
Client
Urvashi Saana (@urvashi_saana)
Tools Used
Canva / Photoshop
Role
Cover Art Designer
OBJECTIVE

Create a single cover artwork for "DEADEND" — a music release by indie artist Saana. The cover needed to communicate the emotional weight of the song title while being thumb-stopping on Spotify's algorithm-driven discovery feeds.

PROCESS

Chose a deep red palette to convey intensity and emotional rawness. Used a silhouetted female figure centered in a red circle/triangle geometry to create mystery and intrigue without revealing the artist directly. Typography: bold slab-serif "DEADEND" anchors the bottom; light-weight tracking on "URVASHI PRESENTS" creates editorial contrast. Spotify branding and pre-save callout placed functionally top-right.

WHY THIS IS THE STRONGEST PIECE

It solves a real business problem (pre-save conversion), serves a real client, communicates a concept (emotional duality, mystery), and demonstrates understanding of platform context (Spotify square artwork). This is the kind of work that belongs at the top of your portfolio.

PORTFOLIO DESCRIPTION

"Cover art for an independent artist's single release — designed for Spotify's discovery context, combining editorial visual language with conversion-oriented platform callouts."
